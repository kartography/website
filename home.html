The study and practice of making and using knowledge and cognitive maps

Grow your knowledge

Knowledge is valuable resource but only if it continually expands and evolves.

Unlike databases, knowledge systems provide a means to grow knowledge using semantic data. Semantic data is meaningful, precise and provides greater insight.

It improves processes and decision making and interconnects different systems providing an effective partnership between people and computers.

Using the ResearchSpace knowledge base system Kartography also addresses

  • Equality and Diversity in data through contextualisation
  • Combining Quantity with Quality
  • Complex, dynamic, and interconnected processes
  • Provenance of knowledge

“If knowledge isn’t challenged to grow, it disappears fast”

Peter Drucker

Data to knowledge transformation services

Kartography can model and transform data into a richer and flexible semantic form that brings out the explicit meaning and relationships hidden in data. This opens up opportunities for continually expanding the model to meet new demands and questions. This work often involves correcting data issues commonly embedded in traditional database systems which requires correction for a precise semantic environment. This consultancy is usually made available when using our knowledge base services.

Knowledge Process Consultancy

Kartography can work with you to understand your knowledge processes providing experience and expertise to inform a new knowledge base. Often existing processes have been influenced to service an existing database system. Kartography can help re-evaluate existing processes to inform and align with a new Since a knowledge base system can evolve over time, this work can be informed by longer term strategies or reviews of existing processes which have been determined by a traditional database.

Maximising Experience

Kartography designs interfaces that reflect thinking processes. They can be changed and evolve to reflect changes and using the ResearchSpace system can allow users to generate their own models of information based on changing knowledge. The ResearchSpace system is designed to bring out the meaning in the data rather than provide its own semantics and this separation allows a flexible system for user experience which can also evolve.
