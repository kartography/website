Data to knowledge transformation

Kartography can model and transform legacy data into a richer and flexible semantic form revealing explicit meaning and relationships hidden in data. This opens up opportunities for expanding the model to meet new processes and questions. This work often involves correcting data issues commonly embedded in traditional database systems. This consultancy is usually made available alongside other knowledge consultancy.

Knowledge Process Consultancy

Kartography can work with you to understand your knowledge processes providing experience and expertise to inform a new knowledge base. Often existing processes have been influenced to service an existing database system. Kartography can help re-evaluate existing processes to inform and align with a new Since a knowledge base system can evolve over time, this work can be informed by longer term strategies or reviews of existing processes which have been determined by a traditional database.

Semantic Experience

Kartography designs interfaces that reflect thinking processes. They can be changed and evolve to reflect changes. Using the ResearchSpace system allow people to generate their own models of information based on changing knowledge. The ResearchSpace system is designed to bring out the meaning in the data rather than impose technical constraints allowing a flexible system that reflects its users thinking.

Knowledge Process Training

Kartography provides standard and bespoke training courses that can help people work effectively with data, regardless of your technical expertise. Kartography teaches non-technical subject experts to understand and make use of semantic link data in their everyday work, and supporting larger scale projects.

Interdisciplinary Humanities & Social Science Consultancy

Kartography draws upon extensive experience of working within Cultural Heritage including the Royal Academy of Arts, the British Museum and the National Gallery. Kartography expertise includes historical and social science methodology and specialisms like Ethnography and Anthopology, Egyptology, Conservation and Conservation Science, and Art History. This allows Kartography to add significant value to heritage and humanities projects providing interdisciplinary expertise. This includes a specialism in knowledge engineering using linked data and ontologies.

The Home of ResearchSpace

Kartography consist of the team that designed and developed the ResearchSpace semantic knowledge system at the British Museum. The ResearchSpace system is an open source system based on 10 years of critical research into the foundations of digital research methods in the humanities. It's design is backed by an extensive empirical invetigation into the use of computers in historical and social research.
